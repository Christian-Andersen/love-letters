August 3, 1944

A brief note — keep it close
My dearest, I write between patrols. The sky here is a steel grey and sometimes I pretend it is the sea and you are on the other shore. There is talk of movement; should I be away longer than expected, know that you are carried in every thought. I pinned a dried blossom to this page — it is pressed flat, but it holds the memory of summer. With all my heart, T.
